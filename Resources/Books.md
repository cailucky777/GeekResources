# Recommended Books for K12 CS Education

A comprehensive list of book recommendations for students and teachers. Books marked with **FREE** are available online at no cost. Many free programming books can also be found at the [Free Programming Books](https://github.com/EbookFoundation/free-programming-books) repository on GitHub.

---

## Swift & iOS Development

| Title | Author | Free/Paid | Difficulty | Description |
|-------|--------|-----------|------------|-------------|
| *The Swift Programming Language* | Apple Inc. | **FREE** ([Read online](https://docs.swift.org/swift-book/)) | Beginner | The official Swift language guide -- the definitive starting point for learning Swift syntax and concepts. |
| *Hacking with Swift* | Paul Hudson | **FREE** ([Read online](https://www.hackingwithswift.com/read)) | Beginner--Intermediate | Project-based learning with 39 real iOS projects; perfect for students who learn by doing. |
| *SwiftUI by Example* | Paul Hudson | **FREE** ([Read online](https://www.hackingwithswift.com/quick-start/swiftui)) | Beginner--Intermediate | Hands-on guide to building SwiftUI apps with practical, copy-and-run examples. |
| *Develop in Swift Fundamentals* | Apple Education | **FREE** ([Apple Books](https://books.apple.com/book/develop-in-swift-fundamentals/id1581182804)) | Beginner | Apple's official curriculum designed for classroom use with guided projects and teacher resources. |
| *iOS Programming: The Big Nerd Ranch Guide* (7th ed.) | Christian Keur & Aaron Hillegass | Paid | Intermediate | A thorough, well-structured textbook that walks through iOS development concepts step by step. |
| *Swift in Depth* | Tjeerd in 't Veen | Paid | Intermediate--Advanced | Explores advanced Swift patterns and best practices for students ready to go beyond the basics. |
| *App Development with Swift Certifications* | Apple | **FREE** ([Curriculum](https://www.apple.com/education/k12/apple-teacher/)) | Beginner--Intermediate | Official Apple certification pathway with structured learning materials for educators and students. |

## Unity & Game Development

| Title | Author | Free/Paid | Difficulty | Description |
|-------|--------|-----------|------------|-------------|
| *Unity in Action* (3rd ed.) | Joe Hocking | Paid | Beginner--Intermediate | A practical introduction to Unity game development with C#; great for first-time game developers. |
| *Learning C# by Developing Games with Unity* | Harrison Ferrone | Paid | Beginner | Teaches C# fundamentals through Unity game projects, ideal for students with no prior coding experience. |
| *Game Programming Patterns* | Robert Nystrom | **FREE** ([Read online](https://gameprogrammingpatterns.com/)) | Intermediate | Explains common design patterns used in game development with clear, visual examples. |
| *The Game Maker's Companion* | Jacob Habgood et al. | Paid | Beginner | Covers foundational game design concepts accessible to younger learners. |
| *Mathematics for 3D Game Programming and Computer Graphics* | Eric Lengyel | Paid | Advanced | Essential math concepts (vectors, matrices, physics) behind 3D game engines. |
| *Unity Certified Programmer Exam Guide* | Philip Walker | Paid | Intermediate--Advanced | Structured preparation guide aligned with Unity's official certification. |

## Computer Science Fundamentals

| Title | Author | Free/Paid | Difficulty | Description |
|-------|--------|-----------|------------|-------------|
| *Computer Science Distilled* | Wladston Ferreira Filho | Paid | Beginner | A concise, illustrated overview of core CS concepts -- perfect for younger students or quick reviews. |
| *Think Python* (3rd ed.) | Allen B. Downey | **FREE** ([Read online](https://greenteapress.com/wp/think-python-3rd-edition/)) | Beginner | An approachable introduction to programming and computational thinking using Python. |
| *Structure and Interpretation of Computer Programs* | Abelson & Sussman | **FREE** ([Read online](https://mitpress.mit.edu/sites/default/files/sicp/full-text/book/book.html)) | Advanced | The classic MIT text on programming principles and abstraction; suitable for gifted senior students. |
| *Code: The Hidden Language of Computer Hardware and Software* | Charles Petzold | Paid | Beginner | Brilliantly explains how computers work from first principles -- highly recommended for curious students. |
| *Automate the Boring Stuff with Python* | Al Sweigart | **FREE** ([Read online](https://automatetheboringstuff.com/)) | Beginner | Practical Python projects that show students how programming solves real-world problems. |
| *How Computers Work* (10th ed.) | Ron White | Paid | Beginner | Richly illustrated guide to computer hardware and software concepts for visual learners. |

## Competitive Programming & Algorithms

| Title | Author | Free/Paid | Difficulty | Description |
|-------|--------|-----------|------------|-------------|
| *Competitive Programmer's Handbook* | Antti Laaksonen | **FREE** ([PDF](https://cses.fi/book/book.pdf)) | Intermediate--Advanced | Covers all essential algorithms and data structures for competitive programming contests. |
| *Introduction to Algorithms* (CLRS, 4th ed.) | Cormen, Leiserson, Rivest, Stein | Paid | Advanced | The gold-standard algorithms textbook used in university courses worldwide. |
| *Algorithms* (4th ed.) | Robert Sedgewick & Kevin Wayne | Paid | Intermediate | Clear explanations with Java implementations; excellent companion to contest preparation. |
| *Guide to Competitive Programming* | Antti Laaksonen | Paid | Intermediate | A focused guide on techniques and strategies for programming contests like CCC and USACO. |

## For Teachers: Pedagogy & CS Education

| Title | Author | Free/Paid | Difficulty | Description |
|-------|--------|-----------|------------|-------------|
| *Learner-Centered Design of Computing Education* | Mark Guzdial | Paid | Teacher | Research-based strategies for teaching computing effectively to diverse learners. |
| *Stuck and Unstuck in Computational Thinking* | Jane Margolis et al. | Paid | Teacher | Explores equity in CS education and how to support all students in computational thinking. |
| *Computer Science K-12: Imagining the Possibilities!* | Doug Bergman | Paid | Teacher | Practical guide for building a K-12 CS program from scratch with real classroom examples. |
| *Teaching Tech Together* | Greg Wilson | **FREE** ([Read online](https://teachtogether.tech/)) | Teacher | Evidence-based practices for teaching programming, covering lesson design and student motivation. |
