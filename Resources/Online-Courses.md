# Online Courses & Learning Platforms

A curated list of online courses and platforms for K12 CS education, organized from beginner to advanced within each category. All links verified as of early 2026.

---

## Swift & iOS Development

### Beginner

| Course / Platform | Free? | Description |
|-------------------|-------|-------------|
| [Codecademy: Learn Swift](https://www.codecademy.com/learn/learn-swift) | Free (basic) / Paid (Pro) | Interactive browser-based Swift lessons covering variables, control flow, functions, and more. Great for absolute beginners. |
| [Apple: Develop in Swift Tutorials](https://developer.apple.com/tutorials/develop-in-swift) | Free | Apple's official guided tutorials for learning Swift and building apps in Xcode. Designed for classroom use. |
| [Swift Playgrounds App](https://www.apple.com/swift/playgrounds/) | Free | iPad/Mac app that teaches Swift through interactive puzzles. Excellent for younger students (grades 5-8). |

### Intermediate

| Course / Platform | Free? | Description |
|-------------------|-------|-------------|
| [Hacking with Swift: 100 Days of SwiftUI](https://www.hackingwithswift.com/100/swiftui/2) | Free | Paul Hudson's structured 100-day curriculum covering SwiftUI from scratch. Daily lessons with projects and challenges. |
| [Hacking with Swift: SwiftUI Quick Start](https://www.hackingwithswift.com/quick-start/swiftui) | Free | A fast-paced introduction to SwiftUI with practical examples and hands-on projects. |
| [Swift by Sundell: Basics](https://www.swiftbysundell.com/basics/#filter) | Free | Clear articles and tutorials on Swift fundamentals, organized by topic. Great reference for students building projects. |

### Advanced

| Course / Platform | Free? | Description |
|-------------------|-------|-------------|
| [Stanford CS193p: Developing Apps for iOS](https://cs193p.sites.stanford.edu/) | Free | Stanford's legendary iOS development course taught by Paul Hegarty. Full lecture videos, slides, and assignments. |
| [Ray Wenderlich / Kodeco](https://www.kodeco.com/) | Paid (some free) | In-depth tutorials and video courses on advanced iOS topics including architecture, testing, and performance. |

## General Computer Science

### Beginner (Grades K-8)

| Course / Platform | Free? | Description |
|-------------------|-------|-------------|
| [Code.org](https://code.org/) | Free | The gold standard for introductory CS. Block-based and text-based courses for all ages with full teacher dashboards. |
| [Khan Academy: Computing](https://www.khanacademy.org/computing) | Free | Self-paced courses on programming (JavaScript, SQL), algorithms, and internet fundamentals. |
| [Scratch (MIT)](https://scratch.mit.edu/) | Free | Visual block-based programming environment. Ideal for grades 3-8 to build games, stories, and animations. |

### Intermediate (Grades 8-12)

| Course / Platform | Free? | Description |
|-------------------|-------|-------------|
| [freeCodeCamp](https://www.freecodecamp.org/) | Free | Full web development curriculum (HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Python, and more) with certificates. |
| [CS50: Introduction to Computer Science (Harvard)](https://cs50.harvard.edu/) | Free | David Malan's world-famous intro CS course. Covers C, Python, SQL, web development. Full lectures, problem sets, and labs. |

### Advanced (Grades 10-12)

| Course / Platform | Free? | Description |
|-------------------|-------|-------------|
| [MIT OpenCourseWare: CS](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/electrical-engineering-and-computer-science/) | Free | University-level CS courses with full lecture notes, assignments, and exams. For highly motivated senior students. |
| [The Odin Project](https://www.theodinproject.com/) | Free | Full-stack web development curriculum with real-world projects. Great for students exploring software engineering careers. |

## Competitive Programming

### Beginner

| Course / Platform | Free? | Description |
|-------------------|-------|-------------|
| [CEMC Past Contests & Resources](https://cemc.uwaterloo.ca/resources/past-contests?grade=All&academic_year=All&contest_category=29) | Free | Past Canadian Computing Competition (CCC) problems with solutions. Essential for Canadian students preparing for contests. |
| [USACO Training Pages](https://usaco.training/) | Free | Structured training program from the USA Computing Olympiad with progressive problem sets. |
| [USACO Guide](https://usaco.guide/) | Free | Community-maintained guide with curated problems, tutorials, and resources organized by USACO division. |

### Intermediate

| Course / Platform | Free? | Description |
|-------------------|-------|-------------|

| [AtCoder](https://atcoder.jp/) | Free | Japanese competitive programming platform with well-crafted problems and beginner-friendly contests. |

### Advanced

| Course / Platform | Free? | Description |
|-------------------|-------|-------------|
| [CSES Problem Set](https://cses.fi/problemset/) | Free | 300 curated algorithmic problems covering all major topics. Pairs well with the Competitive Programmer's Handbook. |
| [Project Euler](https://projecteuler.net/) | Free | Mathematical/computational problems that require creative algorithmic thinking. |

## Tips for Teachers

1. **Start with Code.org or Scratch** for younger or beginner students to build confidence.
2. **Use CS50** as a bridge course for students transitioning to text-based programming.
3. **Pair Hacking with Swift** with Apple's Develop in Swift curriculum for a structured iOS pathway.
4. **Introduce competitive programming** gradually using CEMC past problems before moving to Codeforces.
5. **Mix platforms** -- students benefit from variety and different teaching styles.
